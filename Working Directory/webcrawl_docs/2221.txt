Linda Kay
Professor, Journalism
Graduate Diploma Director, Journalism
Biography    Selected publications    Presentations

Linda Kay's Explore Concordia page
Background
Columnist  and sports writer for The Chicago Tribune.
Page 2  columnist for Montreal's The Gazette.
Freelance  magazine writer for Chatelaine, Reader’s
Digest, Inside  Sports, Newsweek, People, Ladies Home Journal, Ottawa  Magazine.
Educaton
BA  Journalism, Syracuse University
MA  Media Studies, Concordia University
Areas of research
Pioneering  female journalists in Canada.
Courses taught
Winner of the Dean's Award for Teaching Excellence in the Faculty of Arts and Science in 2007, and the Woman of Distinction in Communications from the Women's Y Foundation of Montreal in 2013, Prof. Kay will be teaching Gender, Diversity & Journalism in the  fall of 2016.
Selected publications
Books
The Sweet Sixteen (McGill-Queen's University Press 2012), details the journey that resulted in the formation of the Canadian Women's Press Club. Elles Etaient Seize (PUM 2015).
The Reading List (Hamilton Books, 2005), a memoir, is a reflection upon the nature of truth in reporting and looks at the symbiotic relationships journalists build with their sources.
Chapters in books
Going Deep. Chapter in The Bigger Picture: Elements of Feature Writing for Canadian Journalists, Toronto: Edmond Montgomery Publications (July 2008).
My Mom and Oprah. Essay included in an anthology, The Oprah Phenomenon, published by the University Press of Kentucky, 2007.
